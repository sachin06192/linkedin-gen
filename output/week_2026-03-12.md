### Post 4: Listicle / Tips

5 things I learned after BitNet dropped their 1-bit LLM inference framework:

**1. "1-bit" doesn't mean dumb.** BitNet b1.58 uses ternary weights {-1, 0, 1}. No floating point multiplication at all. The math is just addition and subtraction. That's not a limitation — it's an architecture decision.

**2. The hardware moat is shrinking.** If your model runs on pure integer ops, suddenly you don't need $40K GPUs. CPUs become viable again. This changes who gets to deploy AI.

**3. Smaller models with better inference > bigger models with slow inference.** For 90% of production use cases, response time matters more than benchmark scores.

**4. Fine-tuning is where 1-bit gets interesting.** A model you can run on a laptop is a model you can fine-tune on a laptop. Enterprise teams that can't send data to cloud APIs just got a path forward.

**5. The real disruption isn't the model — it's the deployment story.** When inference costs drop 10x, every app that couldn't justify an AI feature suddenly can. That's not incremental. That's a phase change.

If you're building AI products and ignoring quantization research, you're optimizing for today's costs, not tomorrow's.
